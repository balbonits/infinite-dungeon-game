# Class Lore

Background, identity, and magic philosophy for each class. This lore directly informs how each class's skill and ability trees are structured. See [SKILLS_AND_ABILITIES_SYSTEMS.md](../systems/SKILLS_AND_ABILITIES_SYSTEMS.md) for the full tree breakdowns.

## General Framework

All three classes arrive as **seasoned veteran adventurers**, sent to manage a brand new dungeon solo. They're trusted with this assignment because they've earned it through a career of survival. Similar to Diablo 1's setup — the hero who walks into the dungeon is already capable. They don't "learn" their craft there.

**Skill levels = refinement during dungeon management, not lifetime training.** The character already knows how to fight, shoot, or cast. Leveling represents sharpening that edge against the dungeon's increasingly dangerous threats. Level 0 doesn't mean novice — it means "this is where your growth in THIS dungeon begins."

**Magic is universal.** Every human processes magicules from birth. The difference between classes is how they use them:

- **Warriors** power their bodies with magic — subconscious physical enhancement
- **Rangers** imbue their weapons and tools with magic — semi-conscious matter manipulation
- **Mages** directly manipulate nature and cosmic forces — fully conscious manifestation

See [magic.md](../systems/magic.md) for the full magicule/mana system.

## Warrior

Trained mercenaries from combat stables — academies run by retired warriors who train the next generation to survive and thrive on the battlefield. Warriors jump from battle to battle, leading from the front with shield and sword in hand. Both tanker and damage dealer, they power their bodies with subconscious magicule enhancement — they don't think "I'm casting," they think "I'm hitting harder" and magicules respond.

Warriors are the first to perish in battle. By the time the player arrives at the village to manage the dungeon, they're the last member of their generation from their stable. A veteran adventurer, trusted to run the dungeon solo because they've earned it through survival.

### How Warrior Magic Works

Warriors use magicules subconsciously to enhance their body:

- **Strengthen muscles** — hit harder, lift heavier, resist knockback
- **Reinforce bones and joints** — take hits that would break a normal person
- **Enhance reflexes** — react faster, parry instinctively
- **Project presence** — Mind abilities (Shout, Intimidate, Ugly Mug, Battle Roar) are magicule projections that affect enemies' nervous systems

A Warrior doesn't think "I'm casting a spell." They think "I'm pushing harder" and magicules respond.

### How Lore Shapes the Skill Tree

**Body — "Everything they drilled into me."**

Six weapon masteries reflecting mercenary pragmatism — a stable trains you in all forms because you grab whatever's available on a battlefield. Unarmed first (fight without a weapon before you earn one), then every weapon type. Magicule enhancement is subconscious — muscles, bones, reflexes reinforced automatically.

**Mind — "What a career of surviving teaches you."**

Mental toughness and battlefield presence. Discipline is internal — the focus, endurance, and survival instincts forged by outlasting everyone around you. Intimidation is external — magicule projection powered by genuine killing experience. Enemies feel the pressure of a veteran who's walked out of battles that killed everyone else.

## Ranger

Wilderness dwellers trained to survive in any situation. Rangers fight from the shadows — sniping targets before anyone notices, setting traps before the prey arrives, engineering kill zones before the fight begins. They're tinkerers and inventors, always finding new ways to track, kill, or capture the most dangerous targets, whether monster or man.

Rangers have a one-track mindset: *"Once we set our sights on you, the question is only 'when', not 'if'."*

Their combat philosophy is fundamentally **indirect.** A Ranger doesn't fight you — they end you. From distance, from concealment, through traps, through explosives. Direct engagement is the backup plan, not the strategy. Think the sniper in the ghillie suit, prone in the grass, patient, invisible — one devastating shot from a mile away.

After a long career hunting the most dangerous creatures and men in the world, the player is sent to "retire" and manage the dungeon. But knowing there's still one more hunt out there, they accept the assignment and hunt the floors for the thrill of the hunt. The dungeon isn't a job — it's the ultimate prey.

**Personality:** Rangers name things whimsically. Where a Warrior calls an ability "Smash" and a Mage calls one "Neural Burn," a Ranger calls their stealth ability "Tip Toes." The ability names reflect the class personality — casual, practical, campfire humor.

### How Ranger Magic Works

Rangers are **tinkerers, not scholars.** They don't study magic theory like Mages or use it unconsciously like Warriors. They learn magic through practical experimentation — trial and error.

A Ranger noticed their arrow hits harder when they focus, so they started doing it on purpose. They figured out that pushing magicules into gunpowder makes a bigger bang. They experimented with channeling magicules into trap mechanisms. They treat their weapons and tools as experiments: "What happens if I push magicules into this arrow? It catches fire. Useful."

The Ranger sits between Warrior and Mage on the magic spectrum:

- **Enhanced nerves and brain signals** — faster reaction time, better coordination, sharper senses
- **Imbue projectiles** — channel magicules into arrows, bolts, thrown weapons for elemental effects
- **Enhance equipment** — temporarily boost weapon properties through magicule infusion
- **Create magic projectiles** — minor manifestation (not as complex as Mage spells, but more than pure physical)
- **Infuse objects** — traps, grenades, and devices charged with magicule effects through tinkering

Equipment enhances elemental output but is never a gate — a Ranger can imbue any weapon, but materials with natural magical affinities produce stronger effects.

### How Lore Shapes the Skill Tree

**Weaponry — "The tools of the hunt."**

Every weapon a Ranger carries is chosen for a specific purpose. Not battlefield versatility like the Warrior (grab whatever's nearby) — these are a hunter's curated loadout. Each one solves a different engagement problem:

- **Bowmanship** — the identity weapon. Silent, precise, lethal from distance. "Sniping targets first before anyone notices." The Ranger equivalent of the Warrior's Bladed.
- **Throwing** — quick deployment tools. Knives, axes — things a wilderness dweller keeps on their belt for fast, close-mid range solutions.
- **Firearms** — the loud option. When stealth doesn't matter and you need stopping power.
- **CQC** — the backup plan. Sometimes the prey fights back and gets close. Not the Ranger's strength — their survival tool when the plan goes sideways.

**Survival — "What the wilderness teaches you."**

The tinkerer side. Rangers don't just fight — they prepare, observe, and engineer solutions. "Invent ways to track, kill, or capture."

- **Awareness** — the ghillie suit. How they set their sights. Tracking, sensing, reading the environment, concealment, patience. The "when, not if" starts here.
- **Trapping** — the patient hunter's toolkit. Why fight directly when you can make the terrain do it for you? Snares, tripwires, decoys, ambushes.
- **Sapping** — tinkering taken to the extreme. Explosives, grenades, area denial. A Ranger who figured out that magicule-infused gunpowder solves problems arrows can't.

## Mage

A scholarly group, proud of their creed of advancing magic *"as far beyond as man's imagination will take them."* Mages study everything and have caused a few... "spectacular" explosions around the world. However, there's no denying their abilities to bend nature to their will. They seek knowledge and try to understand everything unto its absolute core.

Due to this unbound curiosity and thirst to learn, the player accepted an assignment to manage a dungeon in the middle of nowhere. They see it as a chance to satiate this "hunger" by plunging fully into the depths of danger, throwing magic spells to their heart's desire. They packed their magic sack with books, beakers, and burners, and settled in the small village — where the townspeople see them as the resident "loon." Always talking to themselves, smelling like they never bathed since arriving to town, but friendly enough to people and does their job managing the dungeon by themselves.

**Personality:** Eccentric, obsessive, friendly but distracted. The absent-minded professor who also throws fireballs. Their naming convention for abilities reflects scholarly precision — "Neural Burn," "Singularity," "Pain Gate." Where a Warrior is blunt and a Ranger is whimsical, a Mage is academic.

### How Mage Magic Works

Mages create phenomena purely from mental models — the most demanding use of magicules. They visualize the effect (its properties, behavior, shape, intensity), direct magicules to manifest it into reality, and sustain concentration to maintain ongoing effects.

The Mage's skill tree is shaped by three fundamentally different types of magic, each with its own source of understanding:

#### Elemental — "I manipulate what exists."

Fire, Water, Air, Earth. Nature manipulation grounded in everyday sensory experience. A Mage learning Fireball is recalling the heat of a cookfire and scaling it up with magicules. The mental model works because the brain already knows what these things look and feel like from a lifetime of interacting with nature. People drink water, cook with fire, feel the air, walk on the earth. This is where every Mage starts — the most intuitive magic. Low-moderate mana cost.

#### Aether — "I manipulate what shouldn't be possible."

Light and Dark as two expressions of one cosmic force — not opposites, but two sides of the same coin. Based on astronomy: the sun is a ball of light whose mass creates powerful gravity. A black hole consumes light, but was born from raw energy exploding as a supernova. Same source, different expression.

- **Light (outward):** Raw energy, creation, forceful restoration. Like welding — fusing broken things together with raw energy and flux. Not subtle, not gentle. A miracle brute-forcing reality into shape.
- **Dark (inward):** Gravity, consumption, compression. The magnetic force that pulls everything toward the planet, the black hole that consumes light. Mysterious — even the Mage can't fully articulate the mental model.

Neither is good or evil. They are the same magicule manipulation observed from different directions — outward release vs inward collapse. A Mage who understands one has the foundation for the other. High mana cost, limited but powerful spells.

#### Attunement — "I understand how magic works inside me."

The Mage's scholarly advantage. All humans manipulate mana internally — Warriors power their bodies, Rangers imbue their weapons. The Mage is the only class that studies this process and consciously improves it. Turning instinct into science. Low-moderate mana cost.

### How Lore Shapes the Skill Tree

**Elemental (4 masteries)** — Where every Mage starts. Nature is the most intuitive source of mental models. Four elements because those are the four things every human interacts with daily.

**Aether (1 mastery)** — The miracle tier. Light and Dark under one mastery because they're one phenomenon. Limited spell count but each spell is powerful and expensive. This is where magic stops pretending to be physics and becomes truly fantastical.

**Attunement (3 masteries)** — The science of the self. Restoration (self-repair), Amplification (brain optimization), Overcharge (pushing past safe limits). The Mage's understanding of their own mana system, refined into practical techniques.

**General magic practice transfers.** Every time a Mage manipulates magicules for any purpose, their brain gets better at the core act of directing magic. Mastery = visualization reps, not innate talent. Specialization is a time choice, not a design lock.

**Mana cost tiers:**

| Category | Cost | Why |
|----------|------|-----|
| Elemental | Low-moderate | Manipulating familiar natural phenomena |
| Aether | High | Bending reality — cosmic-scale mental models |
| Attunement | Low-moderate | Internal mana management, efficiency-focused |
